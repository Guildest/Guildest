# Discord Analytics: What To Track

This bot should prioritize behavior and outcomes over vanity metrics. The goal is to help server owners understand what drives growth, retention, and engagement.

## Member lifecycle

- Joins
- Leaves
- Invites used
- Role assignment timing
- First message
- First reaction
- First voice join
- Time to first activity

## Retention

- D1 retention by join cohort
- D7 retention by join cohort
- D30 retention by join cohort
- Retention by invite source
- Retention by role
- Retention by acquisition campaign

## Activation funnel

- Joined
- Accepted rules
- Got role
- Sent first message
- Joined voice
- Returned the next week

## Channel health

- Active members per channel
- Lurker-to-poster ratio
- Median response time
- Unanswered threads or questions
- Channel fatigue and decay over time

## Conversation quality

- Unique participants per discussion
- Reply depth
- Repeat contributor concentration
- Moderator intervention rate

## Community structure

- Power users vs casuals
- Role-based engagement
- Cross-channel movement
- Which channels create retained members

## Events and conversions

- Announcement posted -> clicks/reactions -> attendance
- Event participation
- Follow-up engagement after events

## Moderator insights

- Activity spike detection
- Raid detection signals
- Toxicity or safety flags
- Ban/kick correlation with channels or time windows

## Creator or business metrics

- Premium conversion
- Support deflection
- Application completion
- Partner performance
- Event performance

## Product framing

The most useful outputs are decision-oriented:

- Where onboarding loses new members
- Which invite sources produce retained members
- Which channels drive retention instead of just activity
- When support or moderator response time is degrading
- What parts of the server are becoming inactive
